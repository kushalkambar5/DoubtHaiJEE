Radius of Curvature: Derivation

To derive the formula for the radius of curvature \( R \) of a curve at any point:

\[ R = \frac{[1 + (\frac{dy}{dx})^2]^{3/2}}{\left| \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} \right|} \]

Consider a point \( (x, y) \) on the curve \( y = f(x) \). The imaginary circle at this point has radius \( R \), and its center is at \( (x_0, y_0) \).

Equation of the circle: \[ (x - x_0)^2 + (y - y_0)^2 = R^2 \tag{1} \]

Differentiating equation (1) with respect to \( x \): \[ (x - x_0) + (y - y_0) \cdot \frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \tag{2} \]

Differentiating again: \[ 1 + (y - y_0) \cdot \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + \left( \frac{dy}{dx} \right)^2 = 0 \tag{3} \]

Solving these gives: \[ R = \frac{[1 + (\frac{dy}{dx})^2]^{3/2}}{\left| \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} \right|} \]
