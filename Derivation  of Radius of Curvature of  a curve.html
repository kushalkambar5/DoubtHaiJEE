Derivation: Radius of Curvature of a Curve at a Point

To derive the formula for the radius of curvature R of a curve at any point:

R = [1 + (dy/dx)2]3/2 / |d2y/dx2|

Consider a point (x, y) on the curve y = f(x). This point lies on an imaginary circle whose radius is R, representing the radius of curvature. Let the center of this circle be at (x0, y0). The equation of the circle is:

(x - x0)2 + (y - y0)2 = R2    (1)

Differentiating equation (1) with respect to x:

2(x - x0) + 2(y - y0) dy/dx = 0
(x - x0) + (y - y0) dy/dx = 0    (2)

Differentiating again with respect to x:

1 + (y - y0) d2y/dx2 + (dy/dx)2 = 0    (3)

Solving the above equations and eliminating the variables gives us the final formula for the radius of curvature:

R = [1 + (dy/dx)2]3/2 / |d2y/dx2|
