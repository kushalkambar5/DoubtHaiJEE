SPINEL COMPOUNDS FOR JEE

These are the compoonds of the form AB2O4
where,
A is a Group-2 or D-Block metal
B is a D-Block metal
O is Oxygen
These compounds are formed by the combination of two different metals with oxygen.
The general formula for these compounds is AB2O4.
The most common examples of spinel compounds are:
FeCr2O4, CoFe2O4, NiFe2O4, MnFe2O4, Fe3O4 etc.


These are devided into two types:
1. Normal spinel compounds
2. Inverse spinel compounds

1. Normal spinel compounds: In these compounds,
A2+ ions occupy 1/8th part of the tetrahedral voids and B3+ ions occupy half of the octahedral voids in the close packed structure of oxygen ions and O2- ions are arranged in a cubic close packed structure.

2. Inverse spinel compounds: In these compounds,
A2+ ions occupy 1/4th part of the octahedral voids and B3+ ions occupy 1/8th part of the tetrahedral and 1/4th octahedral voids in the close packed structure of oxygen ions and O2- ions are arranged in a cubic close packed structure.

Derivation of formula of both types of spinel compounds:

Since CCP or FCC lattice contains net 4 atoms of oxygen, So
Total number of tetrahedralvoids = 2 X 4 = 8 and
Total number of octahedral voids = 1 X 4 = 4

1. Normal spinel compounds:

Since
A2+ ions occupy 1/8th part of the tetrahedral voids so net number of A2+ ions = 8 X 1/8 = 1 and
B3+ ions occupy half of the octahedral voids so net number of B3+ ions = 4 X 1/2 = 2
The formula of normal spinel compound = AB2O4

2. Inverse spinel compounds:

Since
A2+ ions occupy 1/4th part of the octahedral voids so net number of A2+ ions = 4 X 1/4 = 1 and
B3+ ions occupy 1/8th part of the tetrahedral voids so net number of B3+ ions = 8 X 1/8 = 1 and since B3+ ions occupy 1/4th part of the octahedral voids so net number of B3+ ions = 4 X 1/4 = 1
The formula of inverse spinel compound = (AB)BO4
